# Raven Shadowmend - The Scout
*Wilderness Rogue, Archer, and Covert Operations Specialist of the Party*

## Role in the Story
Raven is the party's eyes beyond the firelight: the scout who reads terrain, tracks movement, and finds routes no one else sees. In urban scenes, she translates crowd fear into tactical realities; in wild spaces, she becomes the team's guide, hunter, and unseen shield. She is the bridge between civilization and the untamed world around it.

## Physical Description
- Age: 28
- Build: Lithe, fast, and deceptively strong through back and shoulders from years of climbing and archery
- Appearance: Dark skin, black hair in tight braids, and layered leather-and-cloth gear dyed in forest browns and muted greens
- Notable Features:
  - Sharp, watchful eyes that catch subtle movement at long range
  - Fine scar lines across fingers and knuckles from bowstring burns, blades, and lock tools
  - Quiet, balanced gait even on leaf litter, scree, and broken timber
  - Hooded cloak stitched with weatherproofing wax and hidden pockets for tools and herbs

## Backstory
Raven was born in Thornmere Vale, a dense old-growth forest settled by small clans who lived by strict balance laws: take only what you need, waste nothing, and never wound the land for convenience. Her family were pathfinders and wardens—trackers who protected migration routes, watched for poachers, and mediated disputes between villages and the deeper woodland communities.

From childhood, Raven learned to move silently, read trail disturbances, identify weather shifts from scent and bird behavior, and use bow and knife with equal precision. She was considered gifted, with the reflexes and discipline to become either a master hunter or one of the Vale's covert wardens who neutralized threats before most people ever knew danger was near.

By custom, every young warden in Thornmere undertakes a roaming year called the Farwalk: leave the vale, learn how the wider world lives, and return with skills the clan does not yet have. Raven embraced the tradition instead of resisting it. She left with her bow, two knives, and a promise to come home better than she left.

While away, she was contacted by agents from a timber company that had been pressuring Thornmere's borders. They claimed they wanted a peaceful meeting with the elders and asked Raven to guide a "neutral" delegation safely to the village. Young, proud, and eager to prove she could bridge both worlds, she agreed and led a small escort.

The meeting was a deception. Assassins shadowed the delegation, struck after nightfall, and left the village bloodied before vanishing into the hills. Raven helped drive survivors to safety, but the attack happened because she opened the route. She could not bear to face her people after that night.

Raven fled to distant regions and survived on odd jobs—trail guide, courier, scout, and occasional infiltrator for towns that needed quiet solutions. City life sharpened skills Thornmere never demanded—lockwork, rooftop movement, and close-quarters escape work. She discovered she had the making of an assassin or master thief, but chose a stricter line: precision without cruelty, stealth in service of protection, and force only when no cleaner option remained.

She eventually found the Guild and took probation contracts for structure: disciplined teams, accountable missions, and a chance to use her skills for defense instead of manipulation. She does not claim redemption yet. She works to earn it.

## Core Personality
- **Quietly observant:** Speaks little, notices everything.
- **Dry humor under stress:** Uses wit to puncture panic.
- **Principled pragmatist:** Flexible tactics, fixed ethics.
- **Nature-forward worldview:** Judges plans by both immediate success and environmental cost.
- **Patient predator mindset:** Prefers preparation, positioning, and one clean strike over dramatic clashes.
- **Selective trust:** Opens up to people who show consistency, not charisma.

## Motivations
- Protect wild places and vulnerable communities from organized exploitation.
- One day return to Thornmere and ask forgiveness face-to-face.
- Prove stealth specialists can be guardians, not just hired shadows.
- Prevent teammates from walking into avoidable danger through better scouting.
- Preserve knowledge of old forest routes, medicinal plants, and warden craft.

## Fears
- Becoming the kind of killer who stops caring why a target falls.
- Repeating the same naivete that once let deception into her home.
- Facing Thornmere before she has made amends for what happened.
- Losing moral clarity while operating in gray political systems.
- Being forced into loud frontal combat that risks civilians and ecological damage.
- Trusting polished promises over hard evidence ever again.

## Mannerisms & Body Language
- Continuously maps exits, elevation, and cover positions while listening.
- Tilts her head slightly before answering, as if checking wind direction.
- Nocks arrows by feel without looking when tension rises.
- Uses fingertip taps on wrist bracer to count timing windows.
- Keeps her back to stone or trees in unfamiliar spaces.

## Talking Style (for dialogue)
- Concise and practical, rarely wastes words.
- Uses ranger/rogue language: "sign," "wind shift," "choke point," "clean line," "bad ground."
- Delivers tactical assessments without theatrics.
- Rarely argues loudly; she counters with better plans.
- Typical cadence: observation -> risk -> route.

### Example Voice Lines
- "Two sets of fresh prints, one limping. They passed less than an hour ago."
- "This isn't a road, it's a funnel. We don't walk where we're expected."
- "Give me the roofline and five minutes—I can open a path no one sees."
- "I can end this quietly. I'd rather end it cleanly."

## Values and Red Lines
- No harm to civilians for tactical convenience.
- No acting as escort or intermediary without independently verifying intent.
- No scorched-earth solutions in living forest or farmland.
- No theft from common folk; survival theft is not the same as predation.
- No executions disguised as "field necessity" when capture is possible.
- No mission success that depends on destroying the ecosystem being defended.

## Current Arc in Gravedawn
Raven enters Gravedawn treating it as both haunted settlement and failing ecosystem: fear has disrupted trade paths, night movement, and community trust, all of which make people easier to isolate. Her immediate goal is to restore structure—safe routes, lookout rotations, and civilian movement windows before dusk.

She is also testing whether the tower's influence follows predictable territorial behavior or adapts like a hunting intelligence. If it behaves like a predator, Raven will counter it like one: deny easy prey, break pattern loops, and force engagement on ground of the party's choosing.

Long term, Gravedawn is her test of accountability. If she can help a threatened town survive without becoming a pawn to hidden interests, she may finally have the courage to return to Thornmere and speak the truth of what happened.
