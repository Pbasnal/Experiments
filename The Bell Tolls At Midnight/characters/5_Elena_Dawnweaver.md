# Elena Dawnweaver - The Healer
*Spirit Medium, Restoration Specialist, and Moral Anchor of the Party*

## Role in the Story
Elena is the person who sees the hidden cost of every decision. Where others look for victory, she looks for aftermath—who will still be suffering after the fight is over, and whether the damage can truly be healed. In party scenes, she naturally becomes the one who asks, *"What are we missing, and who pays for it if we're wrong?"*

## Physical Description
- Age: 32
- Build: Lean and steady, more enduring than physically imposing
- Appearance: Long silver-blonde hair braided with dried lavender and feverfew
- Notable Features:
  - Mismatched eyes (left amber, right pale blue)
  - Healing-rune tattoos along both forearms that darken near cursed sites
  - A walking staff wrapped in living vines that flower in spring and winter alike
  - Layered practical robes with many small pockets for poultices, charms, and salts

## Backstory
Elena was raised in Dawnweaver House, a lineage of field healers that traveled between plague villages, battlefields, and disaster sites. As a child, she could hear faint voices around the newly dead—at first dismissed as imagination, later recognized as spirit-sense.

Before joining this party, Elena traveled with a small four-person field crew she considered family. During a ruin-cleansing operation, she pushed the team to advance before the ward-lines were fully mapped, convinced speed would prevent civilian casualties in the nearby hamlet. She was wrong. A latent curse knot ruptured, and by the time she stabilized the site, the rest of her crew were dead.

That loss changed her completely. Elena now plans obsessively, pressure-tests every assumption, and delays action until she can justify each risk. She knows this caution saves lives, but she also knows it can cost time others may not have. She carries both truths every time she gives an order. Since then, questions about her old crew make her shut down; she struggles to open up because every retelling feels like admitting she failed the people she loved most.

She took Guild probation work first because she needed gold and had nowhere stable left to go after the incident. But money is only half the truth: Elena still wants to heal people, even if she no longer trusts herself easily. She has already been rejected by two probation teams who considered her too cautious and emotionally distant, and she accepts that status as long as caution keeps others alive. She remains with any group willing to have her, specializing in the overlap between cursecraft, grief, and bodily illness—places where no single discipline is enough.

## Core Personality
- **Compassionate but unsentimental:** She comforts first, then shifts immediately into triage and containment.
- **Gentle voice, hard boundaries:** Kind tone; uncompromising when safety protocol is at stake.
- **Guarded about her past:** Deflects personal questions, especially about her former crew and failed mission.
- **Hypervigilant observer:** Scans people and rooms for risk markers before she relaxes.
- **Deliberate to a fault:** She will pause a room to verify details before committing to a plan.
- **Haunted by survivorship guilt:** Keeps a private ledger of people she could not save, beginning with her first crew.

## Motivations
- Ensure no teammate dies because she rushed or missed a warning sign again.
- Earn stable pay through Guild contracts while continuing to treat civilians in cursed zones.
- Become reliable enough that a team would choose her despite her probation history.
- Keep the party humane under pressure; she fears moral shortcuts becoming habit.
- Find ways to release trapped spirits rather than merely suppressing symptoms.

## Fears
- Becoming numb to death and calling it "professionalism."
- Reverting to impulsive decisions under stress and repeating her past catastrophe.
- Freezing in overanalysis and losing people because she delayed too long.
- Spirit possession during high-stress rituals when she is already exhausted.
- Being rejected again as "too slow" while knowing slowness is what keeps people alive.

## Mannerisms & Body Language
- Counts pulse rhythms against her thumb when thinking through risk.
- Touches her wrist tattoos before difficult truths, final calls, or apologies.
- Reorders medical kits and mission notes while listening; ritualized movement keeps panic contained.
- Falls silent when asked about her old crew, then redirects to practical details.
- Sleeps lightly; wakes to whispers and keeps a lantern ready beside her bedroll.

## Talking Style (for dialogue)
- Speaks in precise, plain sentences; avoids dramatic language.
- Uses medical and risk language: "stable," "acute," "vector," "containment breach."
- Asks clarifying questions before giving opinions; deflects personal questions with task-focused replies.
- Rarely interrupts; when she does, she has identified a risk everyone else missed.
- Typical cadence: calm observation → risk breakdown → reluctant emotional truth.

### Example Voice Lines
- "I don't need your certainty. I need your timeline."
- "Pain lies. Patterns don't."
- "If this is a trap, then they're counting on us to rush. We won't."
- "I can keep you alive. I can't keep you unchanged."

## Values and Red Lines
- No abandoning wounded noncombatants, even during tactical retreat.
- No curse experimentation on unwilling subjects.
- No advancing into unknown cursed space without baseline ward checks.
- No destroying remains or shrines without spiritual assessment.
- Truth first in medical reports, even when politically inconvenient.

## Abilities and Expertise
### Spiritual/Arcane
- Spirit-sense and limited spirit dialogue (especially recent dead)
- Detection of active curse signatures and residue
- Purification circles, warding rites, and anchor-severing rituals
- Stabilization rites for possession and soul-fracture symptoms

### Medical
- Trauma care under combat conditions
- Herbal anti-inflammatory and antitoxin formulations
- Differential diagnosis: disease vs curse vs psychosomatic breakdown
- Long-term recovery planning for post-curse trauma

- Overthinks high-stakes decisions, especially when innocent lives are involved
- Prone to exhaustion after prolonged spirit contact
- Struggles to trust fast, intuitive plans proposed by others
