# Lyra Steelfist - The Warrior
*Former Military Commander turned Guild Fighter*

## Physical Description
- Age: Early 30s
- Appearance: Muscular build, short auburn hair with a white streak
- Notable Features:
  - Intricate armor with reinforced joints for mobility
  - Collection of various weapon types strapped across her back and belt
  - Distinctive gauntlets with reinforced striking plates
  - Burns on her right arm from a siege fire years ago

## Background
Lyra is the most battle-tested member of the current party, forged by more than a decade in the Crown's Aegis in the frozen North. She earned her reputation the hard way - front-line command, winter campaigns, and protecting settlements that survived only because her units arrived in time.

She was never built for court life.

As her victories mounted, the Crown moved to promote her into a prestigious "strategic advisory" role tied to a powerful noble house. On paper, it was an honor. In practice, it would have made her a decorated figurehead - a noble's sword kept in a velvet sheath, useful for politics instead of people.

Lyra refused the promotion.

The refusal did not stay private. A faction at court, offended by her defiance, began building a case to court-martial her on fabricated charges: insubordination, misuse of command authority, and "operational recklessness" that conveniently ignored her record of successful outcomes and low casualty rates.

Before the tribunal could be formalized, Lyra uncovered the conspiracy through loyal officers in logistics and signals. Rather than stand in a rigged process designed to break her name, she resigned her commission publicly and on her own terms, leaving with honor before the court could touch her.

With her military chapter closed, Lyra traveled south, away from the icebound passes and fortress politics of the North. In the southern territories she encountered the Adventurers' Guild - a structure with less ceremony, faster action, and a simple standard she respected: solve real problems for real people.

The Guild fit her philosophy immediately. No gilded desk. No ceremonial command. No noble leash. Just the work.

She signed on to test it, and this current assignment is only her second Guild quest.

### Tactical Doctrine (Field Reference)
1. **Hammer-Wedge Breach**
   - **Use When:** Enemy line is shield-locked in a narrow street, pass, gate approach, or stair choke.
   - **Tactic:** A triangular assault cell breaks a single point in the line, then rotates outward to widen the gap.
   - **Execution:**
     1. Two heavy fighters form the wedge point with tower shields angled inward.
     2. Lyra follows in the center lane with a bastard sword for high, downward guard breaks.
     3. Rear rank drives with short spears to pin defenders from stepping back into formation.
     4. On the command **"Split"**, outer fighters peel left/right and hold the breach edges for the second wave.

2. **Lantern Net Advance**
   - **Use When:** Fog, snow, smoke, darkness, or dust reduces visibility and causes unit drift.
   - **Tactic:** Advance in linked micro-teams that preserve spacing, orientation, and line integrity.
   - **Execution:**
     1. Squad is divided into three-person cells: point, guard, and anchor.
     2. Anchor carries a hooded lantern (or marked pole) as the reference center.
     3. Every four paces, each cell calls status in sequence; missed call triggers an immediate halt.
     4. Flank cells stay one spear-length from center to prevent overextension and ambush pockets.

3. **Anvil Turn Counter-Ambush**
   - **Use When:** Unit is attacked from side alleys, treeline breaks, or multi-angle raids.
   - **Tactic:** Fix one threat front with a shield anvil while a mobile wing pivots to crush the flank.
   - **Execution:**
     1. Front rank drops to shield wall and holds ground, no pursuit.
     2. Lyra leads two fast fighters and one ranged specialist on a 45-degree pivot into the attacker flank.
     3. Rear line advances only after flank pressure breaks and the horn gives a two-note signal.
     4. Surviving attackers are boxed toward terrain limits (walls, ravines, wagons) for controlled capture or rout.

4. **Rook Step Civilian Corridor**
   - **Use When:** Noncombatants must be evacuated while contact is ongoing.
   - **Tactic:** Build a moving corridor with alternating shield pairs while rotating escorts through it.
   - **Execution:**
     1. Four shield pairs create two parallel lanes: inner lane for civilians, outer lane for defenders.
     2. Crossbow or sling support takes elevated or rear positions to suppress chasers.
     3. At each intersection, front pair advances and locks while rear pair leapfrogs forward.
     4. Last pair collapses the corridor only after headcount confirmation from the route marshal.

5. **Three-Bell Withdrawal**
   - **Use When:** Objective is achieved but enemy reinforcement is incoming or casualties are mounting.
   - **Tactic:** Structured retreat in timed bounds so the unit disengages without becoming a rout.
   - **Execution:**
     1. First bell: ranged line covers while wounded and supplies move to rear.
     2. Second bell: center rank withdraws in two bounds, alternating cover and movement.
     3. Third bell: vanguard disengages with smoke pots/caltrops, then rejoins at pre-briefed rally point.
     4. Final accountability check is mandatory before any renewed engagement.
