# The Exorcist's Field Guide
*A practitioner's reference to spirit taxonomy, remediation, and combat doctrine*

---

## Universal Weakeners
*Apply first, regardless of entity type.*

| Material | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Saltwater** | Sprinkle to define a boundary. Weakens incorporeal forms, disrupts manifestation |
| **Moonbane Tincture** | Sprinkled on ground or thresholds. Weakens spectral presence in an area |
| **Censer smoke** | Cedar and bone ash resin. Forces partial materialisation — prerequisite for combat |
| **Iron shavings** | Scattered on ground. Disrupts teleportation and phasing ability |

---

## Spirit Taxonomy

### 1. The Sorrowful
*Grief-anchored. No malice.*

**Peace:** Complete their unfinished business — deliver the message, speak the name, consecrate the burial.
Ritual: *Grave soil on threshold + spoken name aloud*

**Destroy:** Moonbane oil + Flicker
Consequence: *Location stays melancholic forever. Things never feel right there again.*

---

### 2. The Wronged
*Injustice-bound. Targeted aggression.*

**Peace:** Symbolic justice. Acknowledge the crime over their remains, reverse the original conditions.
Ritual: *Confession spoken over remains + blessed flame*

**Destroy:** Specter oil + Soulfire Edge
Consequence: *Injustice written permanently into the location. Future spirits drawn there indefinitely.*

---

### 3. The Wraith
*Identity-lost. Pure hatred.*

**Peace:** Near impossible. Consecrated ash poured into physical remains plus a binding ritual. High risk, slim chance.
Ritual: *Consecrated ash + full binding ritual*

**Destroy:** Soulbane Extract + Soulfire Edge
Consequence: *No meaningful consequence. Wraiths are too far gone — destruction is the mercy.*

---

### 4. The Cursed
*Externally trapped by another's magic. Suffering, not malevolent.*

**Peace:** Unravel the original ritual. Find the anchor object, apply nullifying salt, reverse the original conditions.
Ritual: *Nullifying salt on anchor object*

**Destroy:** Nullifying salt on blade + Consecration
Consequence: *Trapping spell rebounds violently. Whatever the original caster was containing gets released.*

---

### 5. The Guilt-Eater
*Parasite. Feeds on a living host's unresolved wound.*

**Peace:** Heal the host's wound with healer's tincture — the entity starves and dissolves without combat.
Ritual: *Healer's tincture applied to the host, not the spirit*

**Destroy:** Censer smoke + Soulbane Extract + Consecration
Consequence: *Host's guilt remains unprocessed. A new parasite will find them eventually.*

---

### 6. The Umbra
*A perpetrator transformed by their own evil deeds.*

**Peace:** Mirror ritual — force confrontation with what it did. Place a consecrated mirror before it and compel it to witness itself.
Ritual: *Reflective surface consecrated with nullifying salt*

**Destroy:** Soulbane Extract + Consecration + preparation ritual
Consequence: *Cleanest destroy option. No significant consequence beyond the effort cost.*

## Weapon Oils
*Applied to weapons before combat. Each tier includes the effect of all tiers below it.*

| Tier | Oil | Effect | Availability |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | **Moonbane Oil** | Lets mundane weapons strike spirits at all | Any alchemist — cheap |
| II | **Specter Oil** | Slows phasing, prevents teleportation | Uncommon — moderate cost |
| III | **Soulbane Extract** | Strips resistances, spirit takes full damage | Rare — expensive |
| IV | **Void Ichor** | Damages the entity's anchor directly | Near impossible — ruinously costly |

---

## Weapon Enchantments

### Tier I — Flicker
Faint light on the blade. Any practitioner can cast and sustain it.
Works on: Sorrowful, weak Wraiths
*Casting cost: None. Maintainable indefinitely.*

### Tier II — Soulfire Edge
Cold white flame. Requires training and sustained concentration.
Works on: Wronged, stronger Wraiths
*Casting cost: Wielder cannot sprint or block while maintained.*

### Tier III — Consecration
Blade becomes temporarily sacred. Requires priestly ordination or years of arcane study.
Works on: Cursed, Umbra, Guilt-Eaters
*Casting cost: Drains caster significantly. Cannot be maintained long.*

### Tier IV — Stellarbound Strike
Channels celestial energy directly. Only scholars of astronomy, celestial magic, or ancient rites can attempt it.
Works on: Ancient and celestial-tier entities only
*Casting cost: Physically damages the caster. Unusable by anyone below peak condition.*

## Minimum Viable Combat Requirements

| Entity | Minimum Setup | Without It |
|---|---|---|
| Sorrowful | Moonbane oil + Flicker | Passes through your sword. You exhaust yourself striking air |
| Wronged | Specter oil + Soulfire Edge | You wound it. It wounds you worse. Pyrrhic at best |
| Wraith | Soulbane Extract + Soulfire Edge | Specter oil only slows it — damage does not accumulate |
| Cursed | Soulbane Extract + Consecration | Without Consecration, damage simply does not stick |
| Guilt-Eater | Censer smoke + Soulbane Extract + Consecration | Cannot be materialised, let alone harmed |
| Umbra | Soulbane Extract + Consecration + preparation ritual | Unwinnable. It adapts to everything short of the full combination |
| Celestial Entity | Void Ichor + Stellarbound Strike + astrological timing | The entire system above is irrelevant to it. Do not engage |

---

## The Compounding Problem
*Entities do not wait while you prepare. The system punishes improvisation.*

- **No oil applied** — weapon phases through entirely. No engagement possible
- **Oil only, no enchantment** — you can graze it. You cannot wound it meaningfully
- **Enchantment only, wrong oil** — spirit tanks your hits and adapts to the enchantment's signature
- **Correct combination, wrong entity type** — Flicker on an Umbra does not wound it. It enrages it
- **Preparation interrupted mid-ritual** — partial censer smoke or incomplete iron shavings may provoke without binding

---

## Celestial Entity — Special Case

No combination of standard oils or enchantments can damage a celestial entity. The weapon system above is entirely irrelevant. Engagement requires three conditions met simultaneously — any one alone is insufficient and will provoke without effect.

- **Void Ichor** on whatever ritual implement is used
- **Stellarbound Strike** — celestial magic specifically, no substitution
- **Correct astrological alignment** — the ritual only functions at specific celestial timings

> The entity cannot be improvised against. If the conditions cannot be met, do not engage.
